# Personas — Lot Agent

Derived from the intake options built into the prototype (primary use, timeline, condition, must-haves) — these map directly to real segments rather than invented ones.

---

## 1. Maya, the Overwhelmed First-Timer
**Intake match:** "My first car, learning" · Timeline: "Within a month" · Condition: "Open to all"

- **Age/context:** 22, first job, needs a car for the commute but has never bought one.
- **Goal:** Get a safe, reliable car without being talked into something she doesn't need or overpaying because she doesn't know what a fair price looks like.
- **Frustrations:** Dealer jargon ("CPO," "APR," "doc fee") is intimidating; she doesn't trust her own ability to spot a bad deal; she's afraid of being pressured on the lot.
- **What Lot Agent gives her:** Plain-language intake, hover definitions for jargon (the "certified pre-owned" tooltip pattern), a pre-built negotiation script and walk-away number so she never has to improvise under pressure.
- **Success looks like:** She leaves with a fair-priced car and never once felt confused or pressured.

---

## 2. Daniel, the Budget-Driven Commuter
**Intake match:** "Daily commute" · Reliability + running-cost weighted highest · Budget: monthly payment mode

- **Age/context:** 34, has a firm monthly budget ($450/mo) and cares about total cost of ownership, not sticker price.
- **Goal:** Minimize monthly all-in cost (payment + fuel + insurance) while keeping reliability high enough that he isn't dealing with repairs.
- **Frustrations:** Existing tools (Edmunds, KBB) show specs, not *his* tradeoffs; he doesn't want to manually build a spreadsheet comparing 6 cars.
- **What Lot Agent gives him:** Preference-weighted Fit Score, the "honest tradeoff" callout (e.g., "fuel will run ~$200/mo — $40 over your target, but the agent kept it on top because you weighted reliability highest"), and TCO shown per car in the shortlist.
- **Success looks like:** He picks a car in one sitting because the ranking already reflects his real priorities, not a generic score.

---

## 3. Priya, the Family Upgrader
**Intake match:** "Family hauling" · Must-haves: AWD, backup camera, third row · Condition: "Certified pre-owned"

- **Age/context:** 38, two kids, replacing an aging sedan, wants safety and space without new-car depreciation.
- **Goal:** Find a car that fits car seats and gear, is safe, and won't lose massive value — with some cushion from a CPO warranty.
- **Frustrations:** She has specific hard requirements (must-haves) and doesn't want to see cars that don't meet them; she's busy and can't spend evenings scanning listings herself.
- **What Lot Agent gives her:** Must-have filter chips baked into intake, an agent that keeps scanning "day and night" so she doesn't have to, and a re-plan notification when market shifts make a CPO option suddenly the better math.
- **Success looks like:** The agent surfaces the right call (e.g., "CPO now beats the private-party deal") *before* she would have found it herself.

---

## 4. Chris, the Deal-Skeptical Negotiator
**Intake match:** "Weekend + occasional" · Timeline: "As soon as possible" · Compares multiple cars before deciding

- **Age/context:** 45, has bought cars before, is confident but time-poor, wants leverage more than hand-holding.
- **Goal:** Know exactly what's a fair price *before* walking onto the lot, and have ammunition (comps, headroom estimate) to negotiate down.
- **Frustrations:** Distrusts a single opaque "score"; wants to see the comparison and the reasoning, not just a verdict.
- **What Lot Agent gives him:** The Compare screen (side-by-side pros/cons/TCO), Deal Judge with comp count and dollar delta shown explicitly ("$1,100 under the average of 12 comparable sales"), and a full deal packet with negotiation script and open/settle/walk-away numbers.
- **Success looks like:** He uses the packet as a tool in his own negotiation, on his own terms, and it saves him real research time.
